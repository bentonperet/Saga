## 5. INVOICING & PAYMENT TERMS

### 5.1 Invoicing Schedule

**Mobilization Invoice:**
- **20% of estimated program cost** due upon contract execution
- Supports test equipment procurement, resource commitment, planning phase costs
- Applied against final invoicing (not an additional charge)

**Bi-Weekly Progress Invoicing:**
- Invoices submitted every two weeks based on actual hours worked and expenses incurred
- Invoice includes:
  - Labor hours by resource and hourly rate
  - Travel costs by resource
  - Equipment rental and reimbursable expenses
  - Cumulative program cost tracking
- **Payment Terms:** Net 30 days from invoice date

**Final Invoice:**
- Submitted upon program completion and final deliverables acceptance
- Reconciliation of mobilization payment and all progress invoices
- Release of retainage (if applicable)

---

### 5.2 Payment Terms

- **Mobilization:** 20% of estimated program cost, due upon contract execution
- **Progress Payments:** Bi-weekly invoicing, Net 30 days
- **Retainage:** 5% (negotiable - can be waived with payment security)
- **Late Payment:** Interest at 1.5% per month (18% APR) on overdue balances >45 days

## 6. COST MANAGEMENT & TRANSPARENCY

### 6.1 Program Cost Visibility

PGCIS provides complete transparency into program costs:

**Real-Time Cost Tracking:**
- Live dashboard shows cumulative labor hours and costs by team
- Forecasted cost-to-complete based on schedule progress
- Variance analysis (actual vs. baseline estimate)
- Early warning alerts for potential cost overruns

**Monthly Financial Reporting:**
- Detailed cost breakdown by team, phase, and activity
- Burn rate analysis and trend forecasting
- Change order tracking and approvals
- Invoice reconciliation and payment status

---

### 6.2 Change Order Management

**Scope Changes:**
Changes to the baseline scope may result in cost adjustments, including:

- Equipment quantity changes >10% from baseline
- Additional phases or buildings beyond six 36 MW data halls
- Overlapping L4-L5 phases requiring additional resources
- Extended schedule duration due to construction delays (not attributable to PGCIS)
- Scope additions (e.g., factory witness testing, extended QA/QC, post-commissioning support)

**Change Order Process:**
1. Scope change identified by PGCIS or Hut 8
2. Impact assessment provided within 48 hours (labor hours, cost, schedule impact)
3. Formal change order proposal with unit pricing applied
4. Hut 8 approval required before work proceeds
5. Change order incorporated into baseline and tracked separately

**Unit Pricing Schedule:**
All change orders priced using published hourly rates and travel costs (no markup or premium pricing)

- Resource deployment: Reduced field team commitment while maintaining program management and QA/QC oversight

## 8. COST ASSUMPTIONS & EXCLUSIONS

### 8.1 Pricing Assumptions

**Labor Assumptions:**
- 50 hours per week per resource (non-holiday weeks)
- 25-30 hours per week during holiday periods
- Holiday weeks: Weeks -2, -1 (December 2025), Week 47 (Thanksgiving 2026), Weeks 52-53 (Christmas/New Year 2026-2027)
- No premium pay for weekend work or extended hours (within 50 hour/week limit)

**Schedule Assumptions:**
- Program duration: 60 weeks (construction) + 3 weeks (planning) = 63 weeks total
- No more than 5 cumulative weather delay days per phase (beyond that triggers schedule extension)
- GC turnover packages delivered on schedule
- Equipment available and vendor support per project schedule

**Travel Assumptions:**
- Extended stay hotel rates negotiated for long-term deployment (>4 weeks)
- Resources deployed for full phase duration (no early demobilization unless requested)
- Site access 24/7 without restrictions

---

### 8.2 Exclusions from Pricing

**The following items are EXCLUDED and would be priced separately if requested:**

- **Level 1 Factory Testing:** Performed by equipment manufacturers
- **Test and Balancing (TAB) Services:** Performed by certified TAB contractor (PGCIS provides oversight only)
- **Building Automation System (BAS) Programming:** Performed by controls contractor
- **Vendor Startup Services:** Performed by equipment vendors (PGCIS coordinates and witnesses)
- **Extended Construction QA/QC:** Beyond baseline 25-30% oversight level in Option 2
- **Post-Commissioning Support:** Warranty period services, operational support beyond handover
- **Design Services:** PGCIS does not provide design or engineering services
- **Permanent Facility Staff Training:** Beyond standard operator training during commissioning

## 9. COST OPTIMIZATION STRATEGIES

### 9.1 Value Engineering Opportunities

PGCIS can work with Hut 8 to optimize program costs through:

**Resource Optimization:**
- Hybrid remote/site deployment for management roles (reduce travel costs)
- Flexible staffing during low-activity periods
- Cross-training resources to handle multiple roles

**Schedule Optimization:**
- Compressed testing schedules where feasible (without compromising quality)
- Parallel testing activities to reduce duration
- Strategic use of vendor resources for OEM-specific activities

**Equipment Optimization:**
- Rental vs. purchase analysis for load banks
- Shared test equipment across phases
- Coordination with GC to leverage existing site equipment

**Estimated Cost Savings:** 10-15% through proactive value engineering (without reducing scope or quality)

---

### 9.2 Risk-Based Cost Management

**Low-Risk Elements (Potential for Fixed Pricing):**
- Grey Team labor (predictable weekly hours throughout program)
- Green Team SME advisory (fixed 6-week engagement)
- Travel costs (negotiate fixed weekly rates after mobilization)

**Variable Elements (T&M Approach Required):**
- Blue/Yellow Team labor (dependent on actual testing duration and first-pass rates)
- Schedule extensions due to construction delays
- Scope changes and equipment quantity adjustments

**Hybrid Pricing Model:**
PGCIS can offer a hybrid approach:
- Fixed price for Grey/Green Teams (predictable costs)
- T&M with NTE for Blue/Yellow Teams (protects both parties)
- Unit pricing for scope changes (transparent and immediate)
