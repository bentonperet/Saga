# Hut 8 Riverbend Hyperscale AI Data Center Development - Statement of Work

**Project:** Riverbend Hyperscale AI Data Center Development Commissioning Services (216 MW IT / 300 MW Utility)
**Client:** Hut 8 Corp.
**Document Date:** 2025-11-10
**Status:** Final Proposal

---

## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Project Description

PACHYDERM GLOBAL Critical Infrastructure Services (**PGCIS**) proposes to provide commissioning services for the Hut 8 Riverbend Hyperscale AI Data Center Development, a 216 MW IT capacity (six 36 MW phases) facility with 300 MW utility infrastructure located in Abilene, Texas. The project includes:

- **Switchyard:** 300 MW utility infrastructure (six 50 MW blocks)
- **Data Halls:** Six 36 MW phases with liquid cooling infrastructure
- **Critical Systems:** UPS, battery, transformers, switchgear, chillers, CDUs, cooling distribution
- **Supporting Infrastructure:** Network room, electrical rooms, BMS/EPMS/DCIM/SCADA integration
- **Sequence of Operations (SoO):** Review, validation, and testing support

### 1.2 Service Delivery Options

PGCIS offers two comprehensive service delivery models to meet Hut 8's program requirements:

**OPTION 1: Full Turnkey Commissioning (Primary CxA)**
- Complete L2-L5 commissioning services for switchyard and all six data hall phases
- PGCIS performs all commissioning activities with full responsibility and accountability
- Comprehensive program management, scheduling, documentation, and quality assurance

**OPTION 2: Owner's Agent - Planning, Program Management, QA/QC, and Documentation Support**
- Advisory design review and commissioning plan development/review
- Advanced schedule support with program management and coordination
- Proactive & collaborative quality assurance (Quality in Action)
- CxA oversight and validation throughout L2-L5 activities
- Grey Team (PM, Scheduling, Documentation) - full duration
- Green Team (SMEs) - planning phase with ongoing advisory support
- Blue Team + Yellow Team - L2-L3 QA/QC oversight of switchyard and critical infrastructure
- Optional application of Digital Twin Technology with Machine Learning to expedite commissioning and validation
- L4-L5 commissioning performed by others (GC's team, vendor teams, or separate commissioning firm) with PGCIS oversight

**Design Review Note - CRAH Fan Wall Units:**
During factory test documentation review and technical submittals review, PGCIS will evaluate the specified Vertiv CRAH Fan Wall units (CA606 model).

**Potential Concern:** The CA606 model specified in the RFP may be a European model that could face availability challenges or US standards compliance issues (UL listing, NEC compliance, voltage/frequency compatibility).

**PGCIS Advisory Role:**
- Review factory test reports and certifications for US compliance (UL, ETL, or equivalent)
- Verify voltage/frequency compatibility (480V 3-phase 60Hz)
- Assess lead times and delivery schedules for potential procurement risks
- Provide recommendations for alternative models or design modifications if compliance/availability issues are identified
- Coordinate with design team and Vertiv to resolve any discrepancies early in the procurement phase

**Proactive Risk Mitigation:** Identifying equipment compliance or availability issues during L1 review prevents costly delays and field modifications during construction and commissioning phases.

## 8. ASSUMPTIONS & EXCLUSIONS

### 8.1 Key Assumptions

**Schedule Assumptions:**
- Construction starts Monday, January 5, 2026
- GC provides turnover packages on schedule per master schedule
- No more than 5 cumulative weather delay days per phase
- Holiday weeks (December 2025, Christmas 2026, New Year 2027) incorporated in schedule
- Equipment availability and vendor support per project schedule

**Equipment Assumptions:**
- Equipment list per RFP is baseline (888 pieces per 36 MW phase)
- White Space Option 2 configuration is basis of design
- OEM startup support available per standard terms
- Changes >10% in equipment quantities trigger change order

**Site Access Assumptions:**
- Unrestricted site access 24/7 for commissioning team
- Adequate temporary power available for testing
- Safe working conditions and GC safety program compliance
- Adequate laydown and staging areas for test equipment

**Technical Assumptions:**
- Design documents substantially complete and approved
- Equipment submittals approved and delivery on schedule
- Controls programming complete and ready for testing
- GC quality control program in place and effective
- Vertiv CRAH Fan Wall units (CA606 model) are US-compliant or suitable alternatives will be specified (PGCIS will flag compliance concerns during L1 review)

**Emergency Power & Redundancy Assumptions:**
- **No Reserve Generators Specified:** The facility design does not include on-site standby generators or emergency diesel generators
- **Utility Redundancy:** The switchyard design provides redundancy through dual utility feeds or other utility-side redundancy measures
- **Acceptable Redundancy Model:** PGCIS assumes that utility-provided redundancy (via switchyard configuration) is the accepted backup power strategy for this facility
- **Load Shedding Procedure:** In the event of a utility-wide outage or loss of both utility feeds, PGCIS may assist Hut 8 in developing and validating load shedding procedures to manage available capacity, prioritize critical loads, and execute graceful shutdowns as necessary
- **UPS Battery Runtime:** UPS battery systems provide ride-through capacity for short-duration utility disturbances but are not intended for extended outage scenarios without utility restoration or load shedding

**The following items are EXCLUDED from this scope of work:**

- **Level 1 Factory Testing Execution:** Factory testing performed by equipment manufacturers (PGCIS reviews documentation and may witness testing if optional service selected)
- **Design Services:** PGCIS does not provide design or engineering services
- **Construction Quality Control:** Responsibility of General Contractor (Option 2 includes PGCIS QA/QC oversight)
- **Test and Balancing (TAB):** Performed by certified TAB contractor; PGCIS provides oversight and verification
- **Building Automation System (BAS) Programming:** Performed by controls contractor; PGCIS verifies functionality
- **Vendor Startup Services:** Performed by equipment vendors; PGCIS coordinates and witnesses
- **Permanent Monitoring Systems:** Provided by others; PGCIS validates functionality
- **Operations and Maintenance (O&M) Services:** Post-commissioning facility operations are by Hut 8 team
- **Warranty Management:** Hut 8 responsibility; PGCIS provides warranty activation documentation

**Optional Services (Priced Separately if Requested):**

## 12. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

### 12.1 Commissioning Completion Criteria

Each commissioning level will be considered complete when:

**Level 2-3 Completion:**
- All equipment installations inspected and accepted
- All dead testing completed with passing results
- All equipment energized and operational
- All deficiencies resolved or documented with Owner acceptance
- Documentation archived and reviewed

**Level 4 Completion:**
- 100% of equipment functionally tested per specifications
- All performance parameters validated at design conditions
- System-level testing completed with passing results
- All deficiencies resolved or included in punch list with Owner acceptance
- Test reports submitted and approved

**Level 5 Completion:**
- Integrated systems testing completed successfully
- Multi-system failure scenarios validated
- 72-hour reliability run completed
- Performance metrics validated (PUE, cooling effectiveness, power quality)
- O&M documentation reviewed and accepted
- Operator training completed and competency verified
- Final commissioning report submitted and approved
- Punch list substantially complete (>95%)

### 12.2 Program Closeout

Program closeout will occur when:
- All six phases achieve Level 5 completion
- All deliverables submitted and accepted
- Complete test record archive provided in searchable digital format
- Lessons learned documented
- Final invoicing and contract closeout completed
