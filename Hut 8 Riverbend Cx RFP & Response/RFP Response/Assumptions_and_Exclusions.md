## PURPOSE

This document consolidates all assumptions and exclusions for the PACHYDERM GLOBAL Critical Infrastructure Services (PGCIS) proposal for Hut 8 Riverbend Hyperscale AI Data Center Development commissioning services. These assumptions establish clear scope boundaries, protect against scope creep, and ensure alignment between PGCIS and Hut 8 on project expectations.

---

## SCOPE ASSUMPTIONS

### General Commissioning Scope

**A. Two Service Delivery Options**
- **Option 1: Full Turnkey Commissioning (Primary CxA)** - PGCIS performs all L1-L5 commissioning activities with complete accountability
- **Option 2: Owner's Agent - Planning, Program Management, QA/QC, and Documentation Support** - PGCIS provides advisory design review, commissioning plan development, advanced schedule support, proactive QA/QC oversight, CxA oversight and validation throughout L2-L5 performed by others
- L4-L5 commissioning performed by others (GC's team, vendor teams, or separate commissioning firm) with PGCIS oversight in Option 2

**B. Load Bank Provision and Operation**
- **Option 1:** Load banks may be provided and operated by PGCIS (priced as separate optional service: $50K-$150K for 42-week rental)
- **Option 2:** Load banks to be provided and operated by others
- PGCIS will provide load bank plan review and oversight
- Load bank rental/purchase costs are priced as separate optional service if PGCIS provides

**C. Pressure Testing, Flush & Fill, and Test and Balancing (TAB)**
- Pressure testing, flush & fill, and test and balancing (TAB) of liquid cooling and mechanical equipment to be performed by others
- PGCIS will provide oversight and documentation review
- TAB contractor to be engaged by GC or Owner
- PGCIS will review and approve TAB reports and verify results

**D. White Space Configuration**
- PGCIS will base all pricing and planning on **White Space Option 2** (BASE configuration) per RFP
- White Space Option 2 configuration per phase:
  - 10 Hot Aisle Containment Units (Vertiv SmartRun)
  - 160 Rack Power Panels (1000/1200A, Vertiv)
  - 7 Remote Distribution Panels (400A RXV Panels, Vertiv)
  - 32 Cooling Distribution Units (Vertiv XDU 2300)
  - 36 CRAH Fan Wall Units (Vertiv CA606)
- If other white space options are selected, unit price adjustments will apply

## SCHEDULE ASSUMPTIONS

### Commissioning Timeline

**A. Six-Week Duration per 36 MW Phase**
- PGCIS assumes **6 weeks total duration** per 36 MW phase for L4-L5 commissioning
- **Level 4** (Functional Performance Testing, Heat Load Testing, System Level Testing) = **4 weeks**
- **Level 5** (Integrated Systems Testing) = **2 weeks**
- Total: 6 weeks per phase (40-60% faster than industry standard 8-12 weeks)

**B. No Overlapping L4-L5 Phases**
- PGCIS assumes that **no two Level 4-5 commissioning phases overlap** during the project duration
- Pricing is based on sequential commissioning of six 36 MW phases
- If overlapping L4-L5 phases are required, a change order will be necessary to provide additional resources
- Peak staffing model supports one active L4-L5 phase at a time

**C. Commissioning Continuity**
- Pricing assumes that Level 4 and Level 5 commissioning will be **contiguous** (continuous without delays)
- Any delays not attributable to PGCIS may require a change order for demobilization/remobilization or extended resource deployment
- GC readiness for Level 4 start is critical assumption
- Level 1-3 activities assumed substantially complete before Level 4 start

**D. Construction Delays and GC Readiness**
- Any construction-related delays or GC readiness issues unrelated to PGCIS may necessitate a change order
- PGCIS schedule assumes GC provides turnover packages on schedule
- Equipment energization and Level 3 pre-functional testing complete per schedule
- Weather delays beyond **5 cumulative days per phase** may result in schedule extension
- Holiday shutdowns will extend schedule accordingly (Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year)

**E. Program Duration**
- Total program duration: **63 weeks** (3 weeks planning + 60 weeks construction/commissioning)
- Planning phase: Weeks -2 through Week 0 (3 weeks)
- Construction start: Week 1 (January 5, 2026)
- Final phase completion: Week 60 (February 27, 2027)
- 216 MW delivered at **3.6 MW per week average delivery rate**

### Emergency Power & Redundancy Assumptions

**A. No Reserve Generators Specified**
- Facility design does not include on-site standby generators or emergency diesel generators
- Utility redundancy via switchyard design (dual utility feeds or other utility-side redundancy) is the accepted backup power strategy
- PGCIS assumes utility-provided redundancy is acceptable for this facility

**B. Load Shedding Procedure (Optional Service)**
- In the event of utility-wide outage or loss of both utility feeds, PGCIS may assist Hut 8 in developing and validating load shedding procedures
- Load shedding procedures would manage available capacity, prioritize critical loads, and execute graceful shutdowns
- Priced as optional service if requested

**C. UPS Battery Runtime**
- UPS battery systems provide ride-through capacity for short-duration utility disturbances
- Not intended for extended outage scenarios without utility restoration or load shedding
- Battery discharge testing during commissioning validates runtime under load

### Switchyard Scope

**A. Switchyard Commissioning Assumptions**
- Switchyard commissioning scope includes:
  - Owner-owned 34.5kV primary distribution equipment with MV ring bus architecture
  - 12 MV Switchboards (34.5kV) - 2 per phase
  - 138 Ring Main Units (RMUs) (34.5kV) - 23 per phase
  - Protective relaying secondary injection testing
  - Automated SCADA switching procedure validation
  - SCADA/metering integration and data point validation
  - Grounding system verification
  - Utility coordination support during 34.5kV energization
- **Does NOT include** (unless clarified otherwise):
  - Utility-owned main transformers or utility interconnection equipment
  - Primary injection testing of main utility transformers (utility responsibility)
  - Utility acceptance testing (support only, not execution)
- Six 50 MW switchyard blocks commissioned May-July 2026 (critical path)
- **Option 1:** PGCIS performs switchyard L2-L5 commissioning
- **Option 2:** PGCIS provides oversight of switchyard L2-L3, validation of L4-L5 performed by others

### Integrated Systems Testing (IST) Scenarios

**A. Level 5 IST Scope**
- IST scenarios include:

  - **Mechanical Cooling:** Normal operation, chiller sequencing, buffer tank operation, N+1 redundancy verification, CDU failover, leak detection system validation
  - **Liquid Cooling Integration:** CDU-to-rack integration, flow balancing, temperature differential verification, cooling distribution validation
  - **Control System Integration:** BMS/EPMS/DCIM/SCADA monitoring verification, automated sequences, alarming, SoO validation under integrated conditions
  - **Emergency Scenarios:** EPO testing (if required), fire alarm integration, utility loss response, cooling loss response
- IT load for IST: Realistic load profiles using load banks and/or actual IT equipment
- Performance metrics: PUE calculation methodology, cooling effectiveness, power quality, delta-T verification
- **Sequence of Operations (SoO) validation** under integrated multi-system conditions
- **SCADA monitoring and alarm validation** during integrated testing

## CHANGE ORDER THRESHOLDS

### Scope Change Triggers

**A. Changes Requiring Change Order**
- Equipment quantity variance **>10%** from baseline equipment list
- New equipment types not in baseline list
- Additional phases or buildings beyond six 36 MW data halls
- Overlapping L4-L5 commissioning phases requiring additional resources
- Schedule delays **>5 cumulative days per phase** unrelated to PGCIS
- Work hours **>50 hours per week sustained** beyond baseline assumptions
- Design changes requiring new test procedures or commissioning approach
- Controls system integration more complex than assumed (multi-vendor custom programming, integration issues)
- Switchyard scope significantly different from assumptions
- Extended schedule duration due to construction delays (not attributable to PGCIS)
- Scope additions (e.g., factory witness testing, extended QA/QC, post-commissioning support)

**B. Change Order Process**
- Scope change identified by PGCIS or Hut 8
- Impact assessment provided within **48 hours** (labor hours, cost, schedule impact)
- Formal change order proposal with unit pricing applied
- Hut 8 approval required before work proceeds
- Change order incorporated into baseline and tracked separately
- **Unit Pricing Schedule:** All change orders priced using published hourly rates and travel costs (no markup or premium pricing)
