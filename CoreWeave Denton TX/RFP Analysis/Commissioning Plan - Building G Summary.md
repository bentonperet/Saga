## Executive Summary

This is a comprehensive commissioning execution plan for Building G at the Core Scientific data center in Denton, TX. The plan was prepared by IPS Integrated Project Service for T5 Construction Services and outlines the complete quality assurance, quality control (QA/QC), and commissioning management approach for the 41.2 MW data center facility.

The commissioning objective is to ensure successful verification of proper installation, functionality, and performance of all power, cooling, and control systems to meet Core Scientific's Basis of Design requirements.

## Project Objectives

### 1. Commissioning Execution
- Verify and document proper installation, functionality, and performance of all power, cooling, and controls systems
- Identify and rectify discrepancies, defects, or non-compliance issues during commissioning

### 2. QA/QC
- Establish and enforce rigorous QA/QC procedures
- Ensure all construction activities adhere to approved design specifications

### 3. Commissioning Management
- Provide comprehensive project management services (planning, scheduling, resource allocation, reporting)
- Ensure effective coordination among all stakeholders

### 4. Documentation and Reporting
- Maintain detailed records of all commissioning activities (QA/QC inspections, set-in-place, pre-energization, L3 startup, L4 functional testing, L5 IST)
- Provide regular progress reports highlighting milestones, issues, and recommendations

### 5. Performance Verification
- Conduct thorough performance testing to validate facility meets design criteria and specifications
- Provide recommendations for adjustments or improvements

### 6. Risk Mitigation
- Proactively identify potential risks
- Develop mitigation strategies to minimize delays, cost overruns, or operational disruptions

### 7. Client Satisfaction
- Deliver a fully operational data center meeting or exceeding expectations

### 8. Knowledge Transfer
- Provide training verification and knowledge transfer to client staff
- Enable effective operation and maintenance post-commissioning

## Project Controls

### Planning and Scheduling
- Critical Path Method (CPM) construction schedule used to monitor job progress
- CxPM works with project team, subcontractors, and vendors to gather information on sequencing, logic, and durations
- Schedule is a living document updated monthly but tracked daily
- 3-week Look-Ahead reviewed at each Cx Meeting

**Schedule Assessments Include:**
- Actual completion dates
- Forecasted completion dates
- Changes in planned activities and logic
- Appropriate corrective action
- Changes to the critical path

### Meetings

|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Cx Kick-Off Meeting | Once | On-Site - TBD |

### Information & Document Management

**Document Control Systems:**
- **CxAlloy** - Primary commissioning software for QA/QC and commissioning phases
- **Procore** - Construction document control
- **Email** - General communications and small data transfers (<10MB)

**CxAlloy Responsibilities:**
- IPS CxPM fully responsible for set-up and management
- All QA/QC and commissioning checklists and test scripts managed through CxAlloy
- All QA/QC and Cx documentation submitted and tracked through CxAlloy
- CxAlloy is the sole and final source for all quality and commissioning activities tracking
- Access: https://www.cxalloy.com/ (request access from IPS Cx PM)

**Communication Methods:**
- Email for general communications and small file transfers
- Text message for short messages not requiring tracking
- Phone calls followed by email ASAP to preserve intent and outcome

### Cost and Budget Management
- Standard code of accounts used for cost tracking
- Monthly cost reports reflecting current budget, actual costs, and current forecast
- Cost forecasting based on analysis of invoices, risk register, pending change orders

### Change Management

**Four-Area Approach:**
1. Determine changes are within scope, beneficial, and priced competitively
2. Determine how the change will be implemented
3. Manage the change as it is implemented
4. Determine how to address changes outside scope

**Types of Changes:**
- Budget Changes
- Owner Scope Changes
- Design Definition
- Estimate Adjustment
- Field Changes
- Owner Preferences
- Project Plan Revision
- Random Events
- Scope Changes
- Start-up Changes
