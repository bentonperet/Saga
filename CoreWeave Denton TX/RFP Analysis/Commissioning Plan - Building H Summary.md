### Critical Observations:

1. **Generator Capacity:** Building H has significantly more generator capacity (22.5 MW vs 9.35 MW), suggesting higher critical load or different redundancy strategy.

3. **Fan Walls:** Building H has 10 more fan walls (48 vs 38), suggesting higher cooling airflow requirements.

4. **Transformer Voltage:** Different utility voltage classes (34.5 kV for H vs 18 kV for G) indicate different utility service configurations.

5. **STS Manufacturer:** Building H uses Delta STS while Building G uses ABB STS, though quantities are similar (54 vs 72).

## Building H Unique Commissioning Challenges

### 1. Generator Fleet Management (18 Units)
- **Challenge:** Coordinating startup, testing, and integration of 18 generators
- **Mitigation:**
  - Develop phased startup approach (e.g., 6 generators at a time)
  - Create comprehensive parallel operation test plan
  - Schedule dedicated generator testing weeks
  - Ensure adequate fuel supply for extended testing

### 2. Extensive Busway Network (114 Runs)
- **Challenge:** Systematic inspection and testing of 114 busway runs
- **Mitigation:**
  - Create detailed busway mapping and labeling system
  - Implement systematic inspection sequencing
  - Utilize IR scanning during multiple load conditions
  - Develop comprehensive electrical distribution single-line diagrams

### 3. Large Cooling Infrastructure (48 Fan Walls, 30 Chillers)
- **Challenge:** Coordinating startup and integration of large cooling system
- **Mitigation:**
  - Phased chiller startup approach
  - Systematic fan wall commissioning sequence
  - Integrated thermal storage testing
  - Comprehensive water balance verification

### 4. Power Distribution Complexity (54 STS Units)
- **Challenge:** Testing 54 static transfer switches under load
- **Mitigation:**
  - Develop systematic STS testing sequence
  - Coordinate with generator and UPS testing
  - Ensure adequate load bank capacity
  - Test transfer scenarios under various load conditions

### 5. Different Utility Voltage (34.5 kV vs 18 kV)
- **Challenge:** Different transformer and utility coordination requirements
- **Mitigation:**
  - Early utility coordination meetings
  - Enhanced pre-energization inspections for 34.5 kV equipment
  - Comprehensive short circuit study review
  - Utility coordination for field testing (factory witness testing NOT in our scope)
