## Job Summary

Maintenance Technicians will perform preventive, predictive, and corrective maintenance on all mechanical, electrical, and electronic systems across the Data Center and Hydro Power Plant. This essential role ensures equipment reliability, minimizes downtime, and maintains the highest operational standards in a 24/7 mission-critical environment.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Maintenance Supervisor or Shift Lead
- **Key Relationships:** Plant Operators, Critical Infrastructure Engineer, HPP engineers, data center operations, equipment vendors

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Preventive Maintenance:**
- Execute scheduled maintenance tasks per preventive maintenance program
- Inspect, clean, lubricate, and adjust mechanical and electrical equipment
- Replace filters, belts, bearings, and other consumable components
- Document all maintenance activities in computerized maintenance management system (CMMS)
- Identify and report equipment deficiencies or abnormal conditions

**Data Center Equipment:**
- Maintain UPS systems, batteries, and power distribution equipment
- Service HVAC equipment (CRAC units, chillers, cooling towers, pumps)
- Monitor and maintain backup generators and fuel systems
- Perform infrared thermography and electrical testing
- Support rack installations and cable management

**Hydro Power Plant Equipment:**
- Maintain turbine auxiliary systems (lubrication, cooling, drainage)
- Service hydraulic systems, gates, and actuators
- Maintain generators, transformers, and switchgear (as qualified)
- Inspect and maintain mechanical seals, bearings, and couplings
- Support outage activities and major maintenance events

**Troubleshooting & Repair:**
- Diagnose mechanical and electrical faults using test equipment
- Perform emergency repairs to restore equipment to service
- Replace defective components and test equipment after repair
- Escalate complex issues to engineering or vendor support
- Participate in root cause analysis investigations

**Safety & Compliance:**
- Follow all lockout/tagout (LOTO) and electrical safety procedures
- Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Participate in safety training and toolbox talks
- Maintain clean and organized work areas
- Report all incidents, near-misses, and safety concerns

**Documentation & Reporting:**
- Complete work orders accurately and promptly in CMMS
- Maintain equipment history and maintenance records
- Update technical drawings and equipment documentation
- Track spare parts usage and inventory
- Participate in shift handover and communication

## Required Qualifications

- Technical diploma or vocational certificate in Electrical, Mechanical, or HVAC trades
- Minimum 3 years hands-on maintenance experience in industrial or critical infrastructure settings
- Strong electrical troubleshooting skills (up to 480V)
- Mechanical aptitude with pumps, motors, compressors, and HVAC systems
- Ability to read electrical schematics and mechanical drawings
- Basic computer skills for CMMS and documentation
- Good command of English and Georgian
- Willingness to work rotating shifts including nights, weekends, and holidays
- Ability to respond to emergency callouts

## Desired Qualifications

- Journeyman or master electrician certification
- HVAC/refrigeration certification
- Experience with data center or power plant equipment
- PLC troubleshooting and control systems knowledge
- Welding and fabrication skills
- Confined space and working at heights certifications
- Previous experience with GE, Siemens, ABB, or other major equipment manufacturers
- Russian language proficiency

## Experience Level
