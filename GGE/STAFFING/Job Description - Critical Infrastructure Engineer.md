# Critical Infrastructure Engineer

**Location:** Tbilisi, Georgia

## About the Facility

The Georgian Green Energy (GGE) complex in Tbilisi represents a groundbreaking integration of renewable energy generation and advanced digital infrastructure. This state-of-the-art facility combines a modern hydroelectric power plant with a cutting-edge data center, creating a unique ecosystem where sustainable energy production powers mission-critical digital operations.

Our Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant harnesses the natural flow of Georgia's water resources to generate clean, reliable electricity, while our collocated Data Center delivers world-class hosting and cloud services with industry-leading uptime and efficiency. This integrated approach positions GGE as a leader in sustainable digital infrastructure, demonstrating that environmental responsibility and operational excellence are not just compatible‚Äîthey're inseparable.

## Facility Introduction

Ensure mission-critical reliability at Georgian Green Energy's integrated Tbilisi facility, where a cutting-edge data center and hydroelectric power plant demand unwavering uptime. This unique dual-facility environment requires sophisticated engineering expertise to maintain 24/7/365 operations of essential infrastructure serving customers across the region.

## Job Summary

The Critical Infrastructure Engineer will be responsible for the design, operation, and optimization of all critical infrastructure systems supporting the Data Center and Hydro Power Plant. This essential role ensures maximum reliability, efficiency, and capacity of electrical distribution, cooling, backup power, and building management systems that enable continuous operations.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Senior Infrastructure Manager or CITO
- **Direct Reports:** May supervise infrastructure technicians or coordinate with maintenance teams
- **Key Relationships:** HPP engineering leadership, data center operations, maintenance technicians, CISO, OT Security Lead, equipment vendors

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Data Center Critical Systems:**
- Manage UPS systems, battery banks, and power distribution infrastructure
- Oversee diesel generator backup power systems and automatic transfer switches
- Optimize HVAC/cooling systems for maximum efficiency (CRAC, CRAH, free cooling)
- Monitor and improve Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and energy efficiency
- Ensure N+1 or 2N redundancy is maintained across all critical systems

**Power Distribution & Monitoring:**
- Manage medium and low voltage electrical distribution
- Oversee power monitoring and metering systems (PDUs, branch circuits)
- Coordinate with HPP electrical team on facility power integration
- Analyze power quality and implement harmonic mitigation
- Track capacity utilization and plan for future growth

**Cooling & Environmental Control:**
- Design and optimize data center cooling architecture
- Manage hot aisle/cold aisle containment and airflow optimization
- Monitor temperature, humidity, and environmental conditions
- Implement free cooling and economizer strategies
- Troubleshoot and resolve cooling anomalies and hot spots

**Building Management Systems (BMS):**
- Oversee DCIM (Data Center Infrastructure Management) and BMS platforms
- Configure monitoring, alerting, and automation workflows
- Integrate physical infrastructure with IT monitoring systems
- Generate capacity and utilization reports for management
- Ensure sensor accuracy and calibration

**Reliability & Maintenance:**
- Develop preventive maintenance schedules for critical infrastructure
- Conduct regular testing of backup systems (UPS, generators, fire suppression)
- Perform failure mode analysis and implement redundancy improvements
- Manage spare parts inventory for critical components
- Coordinate planned maintenance windows with minimal customer impact

**Capacity Planning:**
- Forecast power and cooling capacity requirements
- Model infrastructure scalability for business growth
- Plan rack layouts and power density management
- Advise on customer deployment requirements and constraints
- Support sales/engineering on technical feasibility assessments

**Projects & Upgrades:**
- Lead infrastructure upgrade and expansion projects
- Evaluate and specify new equipment (UPS, generators, cooling systems)
- Manage vendor relationships and equipment procurement
- Oversee installation, commissioning, and integration
- Document as-built configurations and update drawings

**Safety & Compliance:**
- Ensure electrical and mechanical safety in all operations
- Implement arc flash mitigation and safety labeling
- Coordinate with HSEQ Officer on safety programs
- Maintain compliance with data center industry standards (TIA-942, Uptime Institute)
- Support facility certifications and audits

## Required Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, or related field
- Minimum 5 years experience with critical infrastructure in data centers, hospitals, or mission-critical facilities
- Strong knowledge of UPS systems, generators, and electrical distribution
- Experience with HVAC/cooling systems and thermal management
- Familiarity with DCIM platforms and building management systems
- Understanding of data center design standards (TIA-942, Uptime Institute Tier classifications)
- Excellent command of English and Georgian
- Ability to work on-call and respond to emergency situations

## Desired Qualifications

- Professional Engineer (PE) license or equivalent
- Certified Data Center Professional (CDCP) or similar certification
- Experience with Tier III/IV data center operations
- Background in power generation or utility interconnection
- Knowledge of energy efficiency and sustainability programs (LEED, Energy Star)
- Experience with CFD modeling for cooling optimization
- Familiarity with EPMS (Electrical Power Monitoring Systems)
- Project management experience
- Russian language proficiency

## Experience Level

**Mid to Senior Professional** (5+ years experience)
