# Plant Operators (Hydro Power Plant)

**Location:** Tbilisi, Georgia

## About the Facility

The Georgian Green Energy (GGE) complex in Tbilisi represents a groundbreaking integration of renewable energy generation and advanced digital infrastructure. This state-of-the-art facility combines a modern hydroelectric power plant with a cutting-edge data center, creating a unique ecosystem where sustainable energy production powers mission-critical digital operations.

Our Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant harnesses the natural flow of Georgia's water resources to generate clean, reliable electricity, while our collocated Data Center delivers world-class hosting and cloud services with industry-leading uptime and efficiency. This integrated approach positions GGE as a leader in sustainable digital infrastructure, demonstrating that environmental responsibility and operational excellence are not just compatible‚Äîthey're inseparable.

## Facility Introduction

Operate Georgian Green Energy's modern Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant, where your expertise ensures continuous generation of clean, renewable energy. This state-of-the-art facility demands skilled operators who can maintain safe, efficient, and reliable power generation 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

## Job Summary

Plant Operators will be responsible for safe and efficient operation of all hydroelectric generation equipment, monitoring plant performance, executing start-up/shutdown procedures, and responding to abnormal conditions. This critical frontline role ensures continuous renewable energy production while maintaining the highest standards of safety and environmental compliance.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Shift Supervisor or Operations Manager
- **Key Relationships:** HPP Chief Electrical Engineer, HPP Chief Mechanical Engineer, Maintenance Technicians, grid operator (GSE), Training Coordinator

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Plant Operations:**
- Operate hydro turbines, generators, and auxiliary equipment per operating procedures
- Monitor SCADA systems, control panels, and instrumentation
- Execute unit start-up, synchronization, loading, and shutdown sequences
- Adjust generation output per dispatch instructions from grid operator
- Monitor reservoir levels, flows, and hydraulic conditions
- Operate spillway gates and flood control equipment as required

**Performance Monitoring:**
- Monitor generator output, frequency, voltage, and power factor
- Track turbine efficiency, vibration, and bearing temperatures
- Monitor cooling water, lubrication systems, and hydraulic pressures
- Identify and respond to abnormal equipment behavior
- Optimize unit efficiency across varying load and head conditions
- Record operational data and readings per procedures

**Safety & Emergency Response:**
- Implement lockout/tagout procedures for maintenance activities
- Respond to equipment alarms, faults, and emergency conditions
- Execute emergency shutdown procedures when required
- Coordinate with emergency services during incidents
- Monitor dam safety indicators (seepage, uplift, displacement)
- Ensure compliance with all safety rules and procedures

**Maintenance Coordination:**
- Coordinate with maintenance teams on equipment outages
- Support troubleshooting of mechanical and electrical issues
- Conduct operator-level inspections and rounds
- Perform minor maintenance tasks as qualified
- Report equipment deficiencies and abnormal conditions
- Participate in planned outage activities

**Environmental Compliance:**
- Ensure compliance with environmental flow requirements
- Monitor water quality parameters (temperature, dissolved oxygen)
- Operate fish passage facilities as required
- Report environmental incidents or permit deviations
- Support environmental monitoring and reporting activities

**Documentation & Reporting:**
- Maintain accurate shift logs and operating records
- Complete operator rounds and inspection checklists
- Document equipment issues and work requests
- Generate daily, weekly, and monthly reports
- Communicate with incoming shift during turnover
- Report generation data to grid operator

**Grid Coordination:**
- Follow dispatch instructions from grid system operator
- Coordinate unit synchronization and load changes
- Provide ancillary services (frequency regulation, voltage support)
- Report unit availability and forced outages
- Participate in grid emergency procedures
- Monitor grid frequency and voltage conditions

## Required Qualifications

- Technical diploma or vocational certificate in Electrical, Mechanical, or Power Systems preferred
- Minimum 2 years experience operating industrial equipment or power generation systems
- Understanding of electrical power systems and generation principles
- Ability to interpret SCADA displays, gauges, and control systems
- Strong problem-solving and troubleshooting skills
- Good command of English and Georgian
- Ability to work 12-hour rotating shifts including nights, weekends, and holidays
- Willingness to respond to emergency callouts
- Pass pre-employment physical and drug screening

## Desired Qualifications

- Previous hydro, thermal, or renewable power plant operating experience
- Power plant operator certification or license
- Understanding of turbine governors, AVRs, and protective relays
- Experience with dam operations and hydraulic systems
- First aid and CPR certification
- Computer skills for SCADA and data entry
- Previous experience coordinating with grid operators
- Russian language proficiency

## Experience Level

**Skilled Operator** (2+ years experience in industrial operations)

**Note:** Multiple positions available for rotating 12-hour shifts (days and nights). Comprehensive training program provided including on-the-job training, simulator sessions, and certification.
