# HPP Chief Electrical Engineer

**Location:** Tbilisi, Georgia

The Georgian Green Energy (GGE) complex in Tbilisi represents a groundbreaking integration of renewable energy generation and advanced digital infrastructure. This state-of-the-art facility combines a modern hydroelectric power plant with a cutting-edge data center, creating a unique ecosystem where sustainable energy production powers mission-critical digital operations.

Our Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant harnesses the natural flow of Georgia's water resources to generate clean, reliable electricity, while our collocated Data Center delivers world-class hosting and cloud services with industry-leading uptime and efficiency. ## Facility Introduction

Lead electrical engineering excellence at Georgian Green Energy's Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant, where reliable renewable energy generation meets world-class engineering standards. This modern facility represents the future of sustainable power generation, requiring exceptional technical leadership to maintain peak performance and reliability.

## Job Summary

The HPP Chief Electrical Engineer will lead all electrical engineering activities for the hydroelectric power plant, ensuring safe, reliable, and efficient generation and distribution of renewable energy. This critical role oversees electrical systems from generation through transmission interconnection, maintaining the highest standards of operational excellence.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Plant Manager / Chief Operating Officer
- **Direct Reports:** Electrical Engineers, Protection & Control Engineers, Plant Electricians, Electrical Maintenance Technicians
- **Key Relationships:** HPP Chief Mechanical Engineer, Critical Infrastructure Engineer, CISO, OT Security Lead, grid operators, equipment vendors

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Technical Leadership:**
- Provide technical direction for all electrical systems including generators, transformers, switchgear, and distribution
- Oversee design, installation, commissioning, and optimization of electrical equipment
- Lead root cause analysis and resolution of electrical faults and anomalies
- Develop and implement preventive and predictive maintenance programs

**Generation Systems:**
- Manage operation and maintenance of hydro generators (excitation, stator, rotor systems)
- Optimize generator performance for maximum efficiency and reliability
- Coordinate generator synchronization and parallel operation
- Oversee generator protection schemes and relay settings

**Power Systems & Grid Integration:**
- Manage high-voltage switchyard and transmission interconnection
- Coordinate with grid operator (GSE) on dispatch, reactive power, and grid support services
- Ensure compliance with grid code requirements and interconnection agreements
- Lead power quality monitoring and harmonic analysis

**Protection & Control:**
- Oversee protective relay systems for generators, transformers, and transmission equipment
- Manage SCADA integration and remote monitoring capabilities
- Coordinate with OT Security Lead on secure access to control systems
- Ensure reliability of automatic generation control (AGC) and governor systems

**Maintenance & Reliability:**
- Develop comprehensive maintenance schedules for all electrical equipment
- Implement condition-based monitoring (vibration, temperature, partial discharge)
- Manage spare parts inventory and critical equipment redundancy
- Lead outage planning and coordination with grid operator

**Compliance & Safety:**
- Ensure compliance with Georgian electrical regulations and international standards
- Implement and maintain electrical safety program (lockout/tagout, arc flash protection)
- Conduct electrical safety training for plant personnel
- Oversee testing and commissioning of electrical systems

**Capital Projects:**
- Lead electrical engineering for plant upgrades and modernization projects
- Prepare technical specifications and vendor evaluations
- Manage electrical contractors and construction oversight
- Coordinate integration of new equipment with existing systems

## Required Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering or Power Systems Engineering
- Professional Engineer (PE) license or equivalent Georgian qualification
- Minimum 10 years experience in power generation electrical engineering
- At least 5 years in leadership role managing electrical teams
- Expert knowledge of hydro generators, transformers, and HV switchgear
- Strong understanding of protective relaying and power system analysis
- Experience with SCADA systems and generation control
- Excellent command of English and Georgian
- Proficiency with electrical analysis software (ETAP, SKM PowerTools, or similar)

## Desired Qualifications

- Master's degree in Electrical Engineering or Power Systems
- Experience with generator refurbishment or uprating projects
- Background in renewable energy integration and grid support services
- Knowledge of IEC 61850 and modern digital substation technologies
- Familiarity with condition monitoring systems and predictive maintenance
- Experience working with international equipment manufacturers (GE, Siemens, ABB, Andritz)
- Project management certification
- Russian language proficiency

## Experience Level

**Senior Professional** (10+ years experience, 5+ years leadership)
