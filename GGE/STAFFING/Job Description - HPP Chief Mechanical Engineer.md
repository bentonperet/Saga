**Location:** Tbilisi, Georgia

## Facility Introduction

Direct mechanical engineering operations at Georgian Green Energy's Tbilisi Hydro Power Plant, where precision engineering ensures continuous renewable energy generation. This state-of-the-art facility demands exceptional mechanical expertise to maintain turbine efficiency, dam safety, and long-term asset reliability.

## Job Summary

The HPP Chief Mechanical Engineer will lead all mechanical engineering activities for the hydroelectric facility, including turbines, governors, hydraulic systems, dam structures, and balance of plant equipment. This critical role ensures mechanical reliability, operational efficiency, and safety across all hydroelectric generation assets.

## Reporting Structure

- **Reports to:** Plant Manager / Chief Operating Officer
- **Direct Reports:** Mechanical Engineers, Turbine Specialists, Mechanical Maintenance Technicians, Welders/Machinists
- **Key Relationships:** HPP Chief Electrical Engineer, Critical Infrastructure Engineer, Plant Operators, dam safety regulators, equipment manufacturers

## Roles & Responsibilities

**Technical Leadership:**
- Provide technical direction for all mechanical systems including turbines, governors, gates, and auxiliary equipment
- Oversee turbine performance optimization and efficiency testing
- Lead root cause analysis of mechanical failures and vibration issues
- Develop comprehensive preventive and predictive maintenance programs

**Turbine & Governor Systems:**
- Manage operation and maintenance of hydro turbines (Francis, Kaplan, or Pelton types)
- Optimize turbine efficiency across varying head and flow conditions
- Oversee governor systems for speed control and load regulation
- Monitor and analyze turbine vibration, cavitation, and bearing performance

**Hydraulic Systems:**
- Manage penstock, spiral case, draft tube, and water conveyance systems
- Oversee intake gates, trash racks, and emergency closure systems
- Monitor hydraulic transients and water hammer protection
- Coordinate sediment management and erosion prevention

**Dam & Civil Structures:**
- Collaborate with civil engineers on dam safety monitoring
- Oversee mechanical equipment for spillway gates and flood control
- Monitor structural health (displacement, seepage, uplift pressure)
- Ensure compliance with dam safety regulations and inspection requirements

**Mechanical Reliability:**
- Implement condition-based monitoring (vibration analysis, thermography, oil analysis)
- Manage bearing systems, seals, and lubrication programs
- Develop critical spare parts strategy and inventory management
- Lead failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA) for critical equipment

**Maintenance Management:**
- Develop and execute comprehensive maintenance schedules
- Plan and coordinate major overhauls and outages
- Manage machine shop, welding, and fabrication capabilities
- Ensure maintenance quality and workmanship standards

**Performance Optimization:**
- Conduct turbine efficiency testing and performance curves validation
- Optimize unit dispatch for maximum plant efficiency
- Monitor and improve heat rate and specific energy consumption
- Analyze and reduce auxiliary power consumption

**Capital Projects:**
- Lead mechanical engineering for modernization and upgrade projects
- Evaluate turbine rehabilitation and runner replacement opportunities
- Prepare technical specifications for equipment procurement
- Manage contractors during installation and commissioning

**Environmental & Safety:**
- Ensure compliance with environmental permits (flow requirements, fish passage)
- Implement mechanical safety programs (confined space, pressure systems)
- Monitor environmental performance (sediment, temperature, dissolved oxygen)
- Lead safety training for mechanical maintenance personnel

## Required Qualifications

- Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering or related field
- Professional Engineer (PE) license or equivalent Georgian qualification
- Minimum 10 years experience in hydroelectric or power generation mechanical engineering
- At least 5 years in supervisory or leadership role
- Expert knowledge of hydro turbines, governors, and hydraulic machinery
- Strong understanding of dam safety and hydraulic structures
- Experience with vibration analysis and rotating equipment diagnostics
- Excellent command of English and Georgian
- Proficiency with mechanical analysis software (FEA, CFD)

## Desired Qualifications

- Master's degree in Mechanical Engineering or Hydraulic Engineering
- Experience with turbine rehabilitation or runner redesign projects
- Background in dam safety engineering and structural monitoring
- Knowledge of computational fluid dynamics (CFD) for hydraulic optimization
- Experience with international dam safety standards (ICOLD, FERC)
- Familiarity with environmental compliance in hydroelectric operations
- Previous work with major turbine manufacturers (Voith, Andritz, GE, Alstom)
- Project management certification
- Russian language proficiency

## Experience Level

**Senior Professional** (10+ years experience, 5+ years leadership)
